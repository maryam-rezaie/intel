1968
Intel Founded

The Genesis

Intel begins its historic journey in California, laying down the structural foundation for global digital transformation and computing history over the next five decades.

1971
First Microprocessor

Intel 4004

The introduction of the first commercially available single-chip microprocessor, enabling billions of calculations per second and fundamentally changing the brain of computer systems.

1978
8086 Processor

x86 Architecture

The birth of the landmark x86 computing architecture. Small changes scaled exponentially, leading to powerful personal laptops and massive worldwide scalability across millions of PCs.

1995
Sustainability Evolution

Decade-Long Goals

Intel formally begins defining long-term sustainability frameworks, implementing multi-decade goals to minimize waste-to-landfill and prioritize community environmental health.

2020
The Big Transformation

Beyond Manufacturing

Intel expands its active environmental scope outside factories directly into internal design business units. The client group targets shrinking component sizes to conserve tons of physical plastics.

Future
Net-Zero & Global Scaling

1.5°C Climate Goal

Actively driving towards greenhouse gas containment, expanding worldwide water-positive status, and engineering next-generation ultra-efficient CPU units to protect the planet.
