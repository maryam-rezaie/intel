1968

Intel Founded

Intel begins its historic journey in California, laying down the structural foundation for global digital transformation.

1971

First Microprocessor

The introduction of the first commercially available single-chip processor, changing the brain of computer architectures.

1978

8086 Processor

The birth of the landmark x86 computing architecture, scaling computing metrics exponentially across millions of units.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in modern high performance multitasking.

1993

Pentium Processor

Intel launches the Pentium processor, bringing stronger multimedia performance and faster personal computing to homes and businesses.

2006

Core Processors

The Intel Core processor family emphasizes better performance per watt, helping shape more efficient laptops and desktops.

RISE Strategy

As part of its RISE strategy, Intel has set ambitious goals for 2030, including driving industry-wide progress in climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

Net-Zero Goal

In 2022, Intel committed to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across its global manufacturing operations (Scope 1 and 2) by 2040.

Water & Waste

Intel conserves billions of gallons of water annually, operating water recycling plants to run circular eco-conscious clean room facilities.

RISE Strategy Blueprint
Our RISE framework sets intentional corporate benchmarks to create a more responsible, inclusive, and sustainable business environment across every link of our supply chains and chip architectures by 2030.
Net-Zero Commitment
Our aggressive net-zero roadmap is designed to eliminate manufacturing carbon footprints across global operations (Scope 1 and 2 metrics) completely by 2040 using 100% renewable power resources.
Water & Waste Preservation
Intel's conservation workflows aim for net-positive water use by recycling over 100% of our production line resources back into native community wetlands and regional ecosystems.
